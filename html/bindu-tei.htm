In the yajnavalkya 4.16 27 (Ed. p. 33), it is located in the centre of the belly, nine finger widths below the waist: kandasthānāṃ manuṣyāṇāṃ dehamadhyān navāṅgulam | caturaṅgulam utsedham āyāmaś ca tathāvidhaḥ ||16|| aṇḍākṛtivad ākāraṃ bhūṣitaṃ tattvagādhibhiḥ | catuṣpadāṃ tiraścāṃ ca dvijānāṃ tundamadhyame ||17|| ``The location of the bulb (kanda) of humans is nine finger widths from the waist. [It is] four finger widths in height and has the same [measure in] length. It appears like an egg, and [it is] ornamented with the five elements of creation and sustenance. In quadrupeds, birds and other beings, it is in the centre of the belly.'' \indent In the hathapradipika2024 3.64cd (gulphadeśasamīpe ca kandaṃ tatra prapīḍayet), the kanda is said to be located near the region of the ankles.
